Chapter 03

Detailed Analysis

Caloric Restriction Mimetics

The field of caloric restriction mimetics has matured significantly. Rapamycin leads with the most robust preclinical evidence, while metformin's TAME trial continues to recruit, aiming to demonstrate that aging itself can be a therapeutic target for FDA approval. [1][3][7][12]

Key challenge: optimal dosing protocols for longevity remain unclear. The intermittent dosing paradigm (rapamycin once or twice weekly) appears to preserve immune function while maintaining mTOR inhibition benefits. [8][15]

Senolytic Therapies

Hit-and-run senolytic regimens — short treatment courses that clear senescent cells followed by recovery periods — show the most promise. The D+Q combination remains the gold standard, with newer candidates (fisetin, navitoclax derivatives) offering potentially better specificity. [4][9][16][22]

NAD+ Metabolism

The NAD+ field has resolved the NMN vs NR debate in favor of NMN for most use cases, based on superior bioavailability data from multiple human trials. However, NR may still be preferred for liver-specific applications. CD38 inhibitors like apigenin are emerging as complementary strategies to boost NAD+ by reducing degradation. [5][10][18][24]

We are witnessing the transition from longevity research to longevity medicine. The next 5 years will determine which interventions cross the translational gap.
