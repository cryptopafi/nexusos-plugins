### If You Were Launching This Agency TOMORROW, Which Report Would You Use?

**Use D3 as the primary decision document, supplement with D4 for depth, and use the Benchmark for the presentation layer.**

Here's why:

1. **D3 is the best COMPLETE package for decisions.** It uses the correct product names, has the clearest market gap identification ($500-2,500/mo), provides specific competitive intelligence (4/33 agencies for GEO, named competitors with pricing), and maps directly to the 14 real products. If you need ONE report to make go/no-go decisions, this is it.

2. **D4 is the best reference document for DEPTH.** When you need to answer "but what do actual practitioners say about this pain point?" or "what are competitors charging at each tier?", D4 has the answers. The Reddit ground-truth and competitor pricing tiers are invaluable for pricing strategy and sales messaging. Its financial models are the most rigorous.

3. **The Benchmark is the best PRESENTATION.** When you walk into a board meeting or investor pitch, the HTML report with visual hierarchy, metric cards, and clear launch strategy is what you show. It lacks substance for decision-making but excels at communication.

4. **Perplexity is unusable in current form.** Despite having the deepest per-point analysis, a truncated report that's missing 40%+ of required sections (financial, regional, prioritization) cannot support any business decision. The content that exists would be excellent supplementary reading for a product team trying to deeply understand customer pain points.

### What's MISSING From ALL Reports That a Decision-Maker Would Need

1. **Customer interviews / primary research** — All 4 reports are secondary research. ZERO first-hand conversations with SMB owners, mid-market CMOs, or enterprise marketing directors. No report can substitute for 10-15 customer discovery calls.

2. **Competitor product demos / tear-downs** — Reports name competitors but nobody actually used their products. A decision-maker needs: "I signed up for GrowthSpree, here's what works and what's broken."

3. **Pricing sensitivity testing** — All WTP ranges are estimates or survey-derived. No Van Westendorp, Gabor-Granger, or conjoint analysis. The difference between "$500-1,500/mo WTP" and knowing the exact price-volume curve is the difference between guessing and knowing.

4. **Channel-specific go-to-market playbook** — Reports say "launch in US first" but don't specify: LinkedIn ads? Content marketing? Partner channel? Cold outbound? With what budget? What conversion rates to expect?

5. **Regulatory deep-dive for launch markets** — D4 mentions EU AI Act (Aug 2026) and GDPR, but no report provides a compliance checklist for actually launching an AI marketing product in the EU or dealing with US state-level AI regulations.

6. **Team/resource plan** — None of the reports answer: "What team do you need? How many engineers, how many account managers, what's the burn rate for the first 12 months?"

7. **Risk analysis** — No report seriously considers: What if GEO/AEO doesn't take off? What if a major platform (Google, Meta) launches a free tool that commoditizes your product? What's the downside scenario?

8. **Actual conversion data** — "15-20% conversion rate" from D3 and "5-8% conversion" from D4 are estimates. No report provides benchmarks from comparable agency launches or SaaS products in this space.
